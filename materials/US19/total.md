# Lexical Context

- An attendee is a person eligible to register for training.
- A training waitlist is an ordered list for a class that has no available places.
- An available place is a seat released or added after the class reached capacity.

# Operational Context

- An attendee may join the waitlist only while registration is open and the class is full.
- Waitlist order is based on the timestamp of successful entry.
- When a place becomes available, the first eligible attendee is notified and has 24 hours to accept it.

# Decisional Context

- First-come, first-served ordering was selected because it is transparent and easy to audit.
- A waitlist notification does not automatically enroll the attendee.
- Coordinators may remove entries that are duplicates or no longer eligible, with a recorded reason.

# Systemic Context

- The Training Catalog provides class capacity, registration status, and eligibility rules.
- The Waitlist Service stores the ordered entries and identifies the next eligible attendee.
- The Notification Service sends the offer, and the Enrollment Service confirms acceptance.
